# Neutrality

This index is operated by [Sato Hub](https://satohub.ai?utm_source=github&utm_medium=index&utm_campaign=onchain-agents), a product company. That's a reason to write the rules down, not to hide them.

## The rules

1. **Placement is not for sale.** No project can pay to be listed, ranked higher, scored better, or featured. Donations fund data infrastructure (pipelines, the verification harness) and buy nothing else.
2. **The score is deterministic.** The [Sato Score](docs/sato-score.md) is computed by an open rubric from observed data — commits, releases, repository state, documentation, provenance-backed listing fields, evidence-gated verification. Same inputs, same score, every project.
3. **Sato's own products are scored by the same rubric,** in the same index, with no home-team adjustment. If a Sato product scores mid-table, it shows mid-table.
4. **Self-reported claims never count as verified.** Verification states (Verified / Audited / ✓ install) are evidence-gated; everything else renders as self-reported or not at all.
5. **Scores are not safety grades.** A high score means a project is open, active, and verifiable — not that it is safe, audited, or profitable. A low score often just means new, private, or thinly documented.
6. **Delisting is factual, not editorial.** Projects leave the index when they are dead (observed inactivity + failed hygiene checks) or misrepresent themselves, not because they compete with anything Sato builds.

## Disputes

Think your entry is wrong — score, status, description, anything? Open a [report issue](../../issues/new?template=report-project.yml) with evidence. We aim to answer within 48 hours, and the rubric, not the maintainer's mood, settles it.
